# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primarily **Azerbaijani secondary-school and university-entrance students** preparing for high-stakes math exams (Buraxılış / graduation and Blok / subject-block exams). They study mostly on phones and laptops, often at home in the evening, frequently under time pressure and exam anxiety. Their job: take realistic **timed practice exams**, get an instant honest score, review every question to learn from mistakes, and track whether they are improving.

Secondary users are **teachers and admins** who author and manage content (exams, questions, classes, tags) and manage users/roles. Their job: publish quality exams quickly and keep the catalog organized.

## Product Purpose

A focused exam-prep platform for the Azerbaijani curriculum. Students get realistic timed exams with instant scoring, detailed per-question review (multiple-choice, open, detailed, matched types), PDF/answer-sheet support, and a sense of progress over time. Educators get straightforward authoring and management tools. Success = students come back to practice again and their scores climb; educators publish exams without friction.

The interface is **Azerbaijani-first** (UI copy is in Azerbaijani, including the schwa "ə" and ç/ğ/ı/ö/ş/ü). Any typeface and component must render Azerbaijani Latin glyphs correctly.

## Brand Personality

Encouraging, energetic, trustworthy. The voice is a supportive coach: clear, direct, in Azerbaijani, treating students as capable. It celebrates progress and effort without being childish or cartoonish. Confident, never sterile; warm, never frivolous.

3 words: **Encouraging. Energetic. Credible.**

## Anti-references

- Generic "education blue" dashboards and the teal-on-white ed-tech reflex.
- Heavy institutional LMS feel (Moodle / Blackboard): dense, gray, joyless.
- Childish, cartoon-mascot ed-tech aimed at little kids. This audience is teens/young adults.
- Cluttered, ad-stuffed test-prep portals.
- Cold corporate admin panels with identical gray card grids.

## Design Principles

1. **Momentum over friction.** Every screen makes the next action obvious and inviting: start an exam, review answers, retry, improve. Reduce dead ends and empty hand-offs.
2. **Calm focus in the exam, energy everywhere else.** The exam runner is quiet, legible, and distraction-free (a student is concentrating under a timer). Landing, dashboards, and results bring warmth, color, and motivation.
3. **Make progress visible.** Scores, per-type breakdowns, streaks, and improvement are first-class, not buried. Honest feedback (including wrong answers) is framed as learning, not punishment.
4. **Confidence through clarity.** Strong hierarchy, generous space, human Azerbaijani copy, no clutter. The student should always know where they are and what to do.
5. **One bold accent, used with intent.** The electric signature color marks action and achievement. It is never decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG **AA** contrast in both light and dark themes (verify text on the coral accent specifically).
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; motion is enhancement, never required for comprehension.
- Color is never the only signal. Correct/incorrect, status, and roles always pair color with an icon or label.
- Fully keyboard-navigable forms and exam controls; visible focus rings.
- Mobile-first with comfortable tap targets (students are on phones).
- Body measure capped ~70ch; minimum body size 16px.
